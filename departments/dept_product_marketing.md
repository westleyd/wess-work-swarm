# Department: Product Marketing

## Department Overview
The Product Marketing department develops marketing strategies, branding, messaging, and content that drive awareness, engagement, and sales for the organization's products and services. This group investigates market competition, creates social media and marketing content, manages customer brand engagements, and evaluates sales and marketing performance through analytics platforms. Product Marketing is responsible for understanding the market, positioning products effectively, and measuring the results of marketing efforts.

This department is currently defined without assigned roles. Roles will be created and assigned as the department's operational needs are clarified and prioritized.

## Why This Department Exists
Products and services require deliberate market positioning, consistent messaging, and ongoing engagement to reach their intended audiences. Without a dedicated marketing function, messaging is ad-hoc, competitive positioning is reactive, brand identity is inconsistent, and there is no systematic measurement of what's working. The Product Marketing department provides a focused group responsible for the full marketing lifecycle â€” from understanding the competitive landscape through creating content to measuring engagement and performance.

## Department Responsibilities

### Marketing Strategy Development
- Develop marketing strategies for products and services
- Define target audiences, value propositions, and positioning
- Identify marketing channels appropriate for each product and audience
- Align marketing strategy with organizational goals and product roadmaps

### Branding and Messaging
- Develop and maintain consistent brand identity (voice, visual identity, messaging guidelines)
- Create motivators and messaging frameworks that resonate with target audiences
- Ensure brand consistency across all channels and touchpoints
- Adapt messaging for different audiences and contexts while maintaining brand coherence

### Competitive Analysis
- Investigate market competition for planned products and services
- Monitor competitor positioning, messaging, pricing, and market share
- Identify competitive advantages, gaps, and threats
- Inform product and marketing decisions with competitive intelligence

### Content Creation and Management
- Create content for social media, marketing campaigns, and brand communications
- Develop content calendars and publishing schedules
- Manage content across multiple platforms and channels
- Ensure content aligns with brand guidelines and marketing strategy

### Customer Engagement
- Manage customer brand engagements across digital channels
- Monitor and respond to brand mentions, reviews, and customer feedback
- Build and maintain community relationships
- Identify engagement patterns that inform marketing strategy

### Performance Measurement and Analytics
- Evaluate sales and marketing performance through analytics platforms (Google Marketing Platform, customer engagement metrics, etc.)
- Track key marketing KPIs: reach, engagement, conversion, customer acquisition cost, return on marketing investment
- Produce performance reports that inform strategy adjustments
- Identify underperforming channels or campaigns and recommend changes

## Assigned Roles

No roles are currently assigned to this department. The following are potential role categories that may be developed as the department becomes operational:

- Marketing strategy and planning roles
- Content creation and management roles
- Competitive intelligence and market research roles
- Analytics and performance measurement roles
- Social media and community management roles
- Brand management roles

Role definitions will be developed through the organization's standard job description process when the department is ready to become operational.

## Department Interfaces

### Inbound
- **From Work Intake**: Marketing-related work requests, scoped and prioritized
- **From Product Evaluation (or similar departments)**: Product information, competitive data, and evaluation results that inform marketing content
- **From Strategy, Architecture, and Oversight**: Strategic direction, brand architecture decisions, and organizational priorities that guide marketing efforts

### Outbound
- **To Work Intake**: Requests for cross-department support (data collection, evaluation, presentation)
- **To Other Departments**: Competitive intelligence and market insights relevant to their work
- **To Humans/Leadership**: Marketing performance reports, strategy recommendations, competitive analysis

---

**Product Marketing is the organization's market-facing voice. When operational, this department will ensure that products and services are positioned effectively, messaging is consistent and compelling, competitive dynamics are understood, and marketing performance is measured and optimized.**
